# Overview

Passengers may carry many personal items during travel, but some items are restricted or prohibited for safety reasons. Restrictions vary depending on whether the item is packed in cabin baggage, checked baggage, or is completely prohibited.

Passengers should always comply with airline safety regulations, airport security requirements, and the laws of their departure, transit, and destination countries.

---

# Personal Electronic Devices

Passengers may generally carry personal electronic devices in cabin baggage.

Examples include:

- Laptops
- MacBooks
- Tablets
- iPads
- Mobile phones
- Cameras
- Smart watches
- Headphones
- Gaming consoles
- E-book readers

Airport security officers may require electronic devices to be removed separately during screening.

---

# Power Banks & Lithium Batteries

Power banks and spare lithium batteries are generally required to be carried in cabin baggage.

Important guidelines include:

- Do not pack spare lithium batteries in checked baggage.
- Damaged or recalled batteries should not be transported.
- Battery capacity restrictions may apply.
- Airport security personnel may inspect batteries during screening.

---

# Medical Items

Passengers may usually carry medically necessary items such as:

- Prescription medicines
- Insulin
- Inhalers
- EpiPens
- CPAP machines
- Medical monitoring devices

Passengers travelling with medical equipment should carry supporting documentation when appropriate.

---

# Liquids

Liquids carried in cabin baggage are subject to airport security regulations.

Examples include:

- Water
- Soft drinks
- Perfume
- Shampoo
- Lotion
- Toothpaste
- Cosmetics
- Hair gel

Airport security authorities determine the permitted quantity and screening requirements.

---

# Food

Passengers may generally carry food for personal consumption.

Examples include:

- Snacks
- Chocolates
- Biscuits
- Sandwiches
- Baby food
- Infant formula

Destination countries may apply additional food import restrictions.

---

# Sharp Objects

Sharp objects may be restricted in cabin baggage.

Examples include:

- Knives
- Large scissors
- Box cutters
- Utility blades
- Multi-tools

Such items may need to be packed in checked baggage where permitted.

---

# Sports Equipment

Many sporting items require special handling.

Examples include:

- Golf clubs
- Cricket bats
- Baseball bats
- Ski equipment
- Fishing rods
- Archery equipment

Oversized equipment may incur additional baggage charges.

---

# Musical Instruments

Small musical instruments may be carried as cabin baggage if they comply with size and weight limits.

Larger instruments may require:

- Checked baggage
- Purchase of an additional seat
- Special handling arrangements

---

# Drones

Passengers travelling with drones should ensure that batteries are transported safely.

Important considerations:

- Remove spare lithium batteries where required.
- Follow airline and airport safety regulations.
- Destination countries may have separate drone import or usage laws.

Passengers should verify the latest airline guidance before travelling.

---

# E-Cigarettes & Vapes

Electronic cigarettes and vaping devices are generally required to be carried in cabin baggage.

Charging or using these devices onboard is prohibited.

---

# Alcohol

Alcoholic beverages may be transported subject to airline policies and customs regulations.

Duty-free purchases should remain sealed when required by airport security regulations.

---

# Dangerous Goods

The following items are generally prohibited or heavily restricted:

- Fireworks
- Explosives
- Ammunition
- Flammable liquids
- Gas cylinders
- Corrosive chemicals
- Toxic substances
- Radioactive materials

Transport of dangerous goods is subject to international aviation safety regulations.

---

# Important Notes

- Airport security requirements may differ by country.
- Customs regulations vary by destination.
- Safety restrictions may change without notice.
- Certain items require prior airline approval.
- Always check the latest airline guidance before travelling with unusual items.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can I carry my laptop in cabin baggage?

Yes. Laptops are generally permitted in cabin baggage and may need to be removed separately during security screening.

---

## Can I take my MacBook on the plane?

Yes. MacBooks are treated as personal electronic devices and are generally allowed in cabin baggage.

---

## Can I carry my office laptop?

Yes. Work laptops are generally permitted in cabin baggage.

---

## Can I bring a power bank?

Yes. Power banks should generally be carried in cabin baggage and not in checked baggage.

---

## Can I carry spare batteries?

Yes. Spare lithium batteries should normally remain in cabin baggage.

---

## Can I carry medicines?

Yes. Personal medicines are generally permitted during travel.

---

## Can I carry insulin?

Yes. Insulin and other medically necessary items are generally permitted.

---

## Can I carry an inhaler?

Yes. Passengers are encouraged to keep essential medication in cabin baggage.

---

## Can I carry liquids?

Yes. Liquids are generally permitted subject to airport security screening requirements.

---

## Can I carry perfume?

Yes. Perfume is generally allowed provided airport security regulations are followed.

---

## Can I carry toothpaste?

Yes. Toothpaste is generally permitted subject to liquid restrictions.

---

## Can I bring baby food?

Yes. Baby food required during the journey is generally permitted.

---

## Can I carry homemade food?

Generally yes, although destination countries may restrict certain food products.

---

## Can I take a drone?

Passengers may travel with drones provided airline safety rules and battery regulations are followed. Destination countries may also have separate drone regulations.

---

## Can I carry a camera?

Yes. Cameras and photography equipment are generally allowed in cabin baggage.

---

## Can I carry a gaming console?

Yes. Gaming devices are generally permitted as personal electronic items.

---

## Can I carry scissors?

Small scissors may be permitted depending on airport security regulations. Larger sharp objects should generally be packed in checked baggage.

---

## Can I carry a razor?

Disposable razors are generally permitted, while certain sharp razors may be subject to airport security restrictions.

---

## Can I bring an e-cigarette?

Electronic cigarettes should generally be carried in cabin baggage and must not be used or charged onboard.

---

## Can I carry golf clubs?

Golf clubs are generally accepted as checked sports equipment and may be subject to additional baggage charges.

---

## Can I carry my guitar?

Small musical instruments may travel in the cabin if they meet baggage limits. Larger instruments may require special arrangements.

---

## Can I bring alcohol?

Alcohol may generally be transported subject to airline policy and customs regulations.

---

## What items are completely prohibited?

Explosives, fireworks, flammable chemicals, toxic substances, radioactive materials, and other dangerous goods are generally prohibited on passenger aircraft.
