# Overview

Emirates provides baggage allowances based on the passenger's travel class and route. Baggage rules include cabin baggage, checked baggage, special baggage, excess baggage, restricted items, and dangerous goods.

Passengers should always ensure that their baggage complies with airline weight and size limits before arriving at the airport.

---

# Cabin Baggage

## Economy Class

Passengers travelling in Economy Class may carry:

- One cabin bag
- Maximum weight: 7 kg
- Maximum dimensions: 55 × 38 × 22 cm

The bag should fit in the overhead storage compartment.

---

## Premium Economy

Passengers travelling in Premium Economy may carry:

- One cabin bag
- Maximum weight: 10 kg
- Maximum dimensions: 55 × 38 × 22 cm

---

## Business Class

Passengers travelling in Business Class may carry two cabin items:

- One briefcase
- One handbag or one garment bag

General limits:

- Briefcase: up to 45 × 35 × 20 cm
- Handbag: up to 55 × 38 × 22 cm
- Garment bag: folded thickness should not exceed 20 cm
- Each item should not exceed 7 kg.

---

## First Class

First Class passengers receive the same cabin baggage allowance as Business Class.

---

# Checked Baggage

Checked baggage allowance depends on the travel class booked.

Emirates uses the Weight Concept for checked baggage on this system:

| Travel Class | Checked Allowance | Excess Fee (per kg over) |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Economy | 23 kg | ₹1000 |
| Premium Economy | 35 kg | ₹1000 |
| Business Class | 40 kg | ₹1000 |
| First Class | 50 kg | ₹1000 |

Important rules:

- Individual bags must not exceed 32 kg regardless of total allowance.
- Total dimensions of each checked bag should generally not exceed 203 cm (length + width + height).
- Any weight above the allowance for your travel class is charged at ₹1000 per kilogram.

---

# Excess Baggage

Passengers travelling with baggage exceeding their free allowance will be charged ₹1000 per kilogram over their travel class's checked allowance (see table above).

Important notes:

- The excess fee is calculated automatically based on travel class and total baggage weight.
- Charges depend on how much your baggage exceeds your class's free allowance.

# Special Baggage

## Sports Equipment

Many sports items may be accepted as checked baggage if they comply with Emirates baggage rules.

Examples include:

- Golf clubs
- Skis
- Surfboards
- Bicycles
- Cricket equipment

Oversized equipment may incur additional charges beyond the standard excess baggage fee.

---

## Musical Instruments

Small musical instruments may be carried as cabin baggage if they meet cabin baggage size and weight limits.

Larger instruments should be checked or transported using a purchased seat when applicable.

---

## Baby Items

Passengers travelling with infants may generally carry essential baby items such as:

- Baby food during the flight
- Foldable stroller or carrycot (subject to cabin space)

If cabin storage is unavailable, these items may be checked.

---

## Mobility Equipment

Wheelchairs and mobility aids are accepted according to Emirates assistance policies.

Passengers requiring mobility assistance are encouraged to notify the airline before travel.

---

# Restricted and Dangerous Goods

Certain items are prohibited or restricted for safety reasons.

Examples include:

- Explosives
- Flammable liquids
- Fireworks
- Toxic substances
- Compressed gases
- Corrosive chemicals

Lithium batteries and power banks must generally be carried in cabin baggage rather than checked baggage, subject to airline safety regulations.

Passengers should review current dangerous goods regulations before travelling.

---

# Smart Bags

Smart bags containing lithium batteries are permitted only when they comply with Emirates safety requirements.

If the battery cannot remain safely installed, passengers may be required to remove it before checking the bag.

---

# Important Notes

- Baggage allowances are determined by travel class as shown above.
- Codeshare flights may follow baggage rules of another airline.
- Airport security regulations may impose additional restrictions.
- Final baggage eligibility is determined during check-in.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can I carry two cabin bags?

Only Business Class and First Class passengers are generally permitted two cabin items. Economy and Premium Economy passengers are usually allowed one cabin bag.

---

## What happens if my bag exceeds the weight limit?

The passenger will be charged ₹1000 per kilogram over their travel class's checked allowance.

---

## Can I carry sports equipment?

Yes. Sports equipment is generally accepted, although oversized or overweight equipment may incur additional fees.

---

## Are power banks allowed?

Power banks should generally be carried in cabin baggage and should not be packed in checked baggage.

---

## What is the checked baggage allowance for my travel class?

- Economy: 23 kg
- Premium Economy: 35 kg
- Business Class: 40 kg
- First Class: 50 kg

Any weight above this is charged at ₹1000 per kilogram excess.
